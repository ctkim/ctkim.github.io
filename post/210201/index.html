Finding My Niche on Clubhouse

CHRISTINE EUNJI KIM
Feb 1, 2021 3 min read

After a confusing first week, I think I’ve figured it out.

Clubhouse is a new invite-only app that uses voice-chat rooms to connect people around the globe. It’s been picked up by people from all walks of life and is a source of infinite connections you’d never have made in real life.

I was invited to join by a friend who was moderating a room that would discuss AAPI Representation in Film. He thought I would add a valuable voice to the topic at hand. At the time, I only vaguely understood how the app worked. I hadn’t even downloaded it yet!

I fumbled my way through the app in the beginning, pressing all the buttons I could. Even though the community on Clubhouse was much smaller than the big social media names like Facebook or Twitter, it still felt unknowably large.

However, from the first discussion I participated in, I was hooked.

My heart raced as I “raised my hand” in the room, and was invited as a “speaker” on the “stage”. I wasn’t quite aware of the ettiquette yet, but the past year of Zoom meetings prepared me for muting my mic.

The conversation was more fascinating than I could have thought, and I couldn’t believe that I was up there, engaging with all these wonderful speakers. The few times I unmuted to say my piece, my heart was in my throat. Thankfully, it being a voice-only chat, the rest of them couldn’t see the panic on my face.

I was confused to see some of the other speakers’ mute buttons blinking on and off while I spoke. I figured it was a way to indicate that they wanted to speak next. Turns out that’s how you indicate applause in Clubhouse! (Sidenote: Am I the only one who thinks of it as snapping?)

I won’t go too much into the in-depth conversation Asian American industry professionals had about our represetation in media. It definitely went to some interesting, and possibly challenging places. Mad props to the Strong Asian Leads club, for always leading conversations such as that.

I quickly joined so many (too many) other clubs. It’s exhilarating to speak to people around the world just about anything in just about any language. (Definitely giving my Spanish conversation skills a chance to shine!)

In the coming post-pandemic world, I imagine that social spaces may take a different shape. Everyone will be so much more open-minded to online interactions and I think that will bring new and exciting networking opportunities. Clubhouse may only be the beginning, and I’m thrilled to be a part of it.

I can’t wait for what comes next.

Clubhouse is currently available for download only on iOS.

CHRISTINE EUNJI KIM

I’m a Korean American actress and filmmaker based in NYC. When I’m not auditioning, filming, or doing karaoke in my shower, I write posts about my acting journey.
