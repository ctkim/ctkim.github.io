Tired Of Virtual Film Shoots?

CHRISTINE EUNJI KIM
Dec 12, 2020 2 min read

A little creativity can make a remote film look like it’s in-person.

Earlier this year, I talked about filmmaking during the pandemic. And it got me wondering: How can I create a short film with other actors remotely?

Of course we’re all familiar with remote filming by now. Even stories that are told completely inside a computer screen existed long before the pandemic. But this method of virtual storytelling has become a crutch for writers during these times.

I wanted to break that mold.

How can I film a short, with other actors, without ever being in the same location as them? How can I use film magic to look as if we shared the same space?

All it took was a little bit of creativity.

I wrote and directed between|us, a short film about two sisters struggling to communicate. I worked with Nicole Jung as my co-lead, and had Kvlkar go above and beyond with the sountrack and sound design. All filmed virtually, no handshakes required.

However, the final product looks as if we shared the same space. And that’s the beauty of filmmaking. I wrote a story that involved a common prop: a door. I planned out the shots and communicated with Nicole what I’d need from her. I edited the final footage together to combine our separate footage seamlessly.

And this is how it turned out.
